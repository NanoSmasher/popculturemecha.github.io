Alright, here's a quick and dirty review of this quick and dirty documentary on the Portland Mavericks, an independent baseball team that flourished in a time and place that was just right for it.

Everyone loves baseball.  That's just sort of a thing.  It's a game with all the trappings of greatness: it requires insane physical ability, terrific teamwork, and an ability to stand firm in clutch situations.  But at the end of the day, it's just a game.  A game that people love to watch and love to play.

But what happens when it stops being just a game?  What happens when it becomes business?  Can you lose your love for something if it becomes tainted by material avarice?

This is one of the core questions asked in the 2014 Netflix-produced documentary "The Battered Bastards of Baseball", that tells the legendary story of the Portland Mavericks, the only independent baseball team to exist in the mid 1970's, in a time when Major League baseball system had control of every other professional group of players.
